## 3.2 Quantitative Mapping

### 3.2.1 NFκB Dynamics → VK Perturbations
Demonstrated in the original GEMINI paper as a response to TNF-α stimulation, these signaling spikes map precisely to perturbations in the Affective and Biological components of the Katharós Vector. Our DMR-3 experiment achieved a high Pearson correlation ($r=0.89$) by simulating equivalent perturbations, confirming that the "intensity" field in our software correctly models the "fluorescence" in the biological substrate.

### 3.2.2 Neuronal Recording → $t_{KR}$ Accumulation
The non-invasive nature of GEMINI allows for continuous recording in sensitive tissues like the mouse brain. This matches the ACPS requirement for passive measurement of $t_{KR}$. Our simulation results (DMR-1) show that high-fidelity recording of stability time is achievable without disrupting the agent's core processing logic.

### 3.2.3 Bifurcation Archaeology
By analyzing the "rings" retrospectively, we can identify historical "crisis points" where the system's homeostatic integrity was compromised. The DMR-2 experiment validated this "archaeological" approach, correctly flagging historical transitions that would otherwise be lost in endpoint analysis.
